Cal Poly Pomona Northrop Grumman Project 2024-25

Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) Electrical Sub-Team Member

Electrical Wiring & Wiring Harness KrakenSDR Radio Frequency Search Device Linux/Ubuntu Raspberry Pi 5 DC-to-DC Buck Converters Circuit Analysis Electrical Troubleshooting PCB Altium Design

The Northrop Grumman Collaboration Project (NGCP) is an interdisciplinary initiative at Cal Poly Pomona that focuses on the design, development, and optimization of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs). As part of the Electrical Sub-Team, participants engage in system integration, electrical component design, and sensor calibration to enhance UAV performance and reliability.

For my role as an electrical sub-team member I helped troubleshoot and debug operation of the KrakenSDR radio frequency search device using Linux/Ubuntu on a Raspberry Pi 5. Furthermore, I designed the schematic and PCB layout of a 5V DC-to-DC Buck Converter using the TPS53515 IC (see pictures below) to power the KrakenSDR, Raspberry Pi 5, and the Pixhawk Orange Cube Autonomous Flight Controller.

5V Linear Regulator Alitum Design Project

Alitum Design PCB Schematic Circuit Analysis PCB Layout PCB Assembly PCB Testing Soldering

In this project, I designed a 5V Linear Regulator using Alitum Design. The regulator is a simple circuit that converts a Vin(min) >= 7V or Vin(max) >= 35V input to a 5V output.

UPDATE: 1/12/25 - Finished soldering the 5V Linear Regulator components and began testing the circuit. Unexpectedly, the circuit did not function as intended. With a 9V battery input, the output voltage was less than 5V. I will be testing the circuit again to determine the issue.

UPDATE: 2/6/25 - Tested the circuit again and found success! The 5V Linear Regulator is now working as intended at Vin = 15V and Vout = 5V. Please see images below.
